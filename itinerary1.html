8 Day Uganda Primate Safari

Gorilla Trekking and Chimpanzee

Safari Details

This Uganda Primate Safari highlights gorilla trekking and chimpanzee trekking as the main activities. This trip will bring you face to face with these great apes in the middle of the rain forests i.e. Kibale Forest and Bwindi impenetrable forest. In addition, you have an opportunity to visit Queen Elizabeth national park for great scenery and wildlife both on a boat safari and during a game drive.

  • Nature walk in Bigodi Swamp
  • Game drives
  • Boat cruise
  • Crater Lakes
  • Gorilla tracking

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What's the difficulty level of gorilla trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest?

    Gorilla trekking in Bwindi ranges from moderately challenging to strenuous, depending on the gorilla group location. Treks typically last 2-8 hours through steep, dense rainforest terrain at altitudes of 1,500-2,500 meters. Pro Tips: (a)-Start physical preparation (walking/hiking) 2 months prior (b)-Pack lightweight but warm layers as temperatures vary (c)-We provide walking sticks; proper hiking boots are essential

  • How close will we get to the chimpanzees during habituation?

    Our chimpanzee habituation experience in Kibale Forest allows observation from 8-10 meters (26-33 feet), the closest permitted distance in Uganda. The process involves following researchers as they document chimp behavior, offering more intimate viewing than standard tracking. NOTE: Timing Matters Morning treks (7:30-11:30) offer best activity views as chimps feed, while afternoon habituation (14:00-17:00) often features nesting behaviors.

  • What health precautions are required for primate encounters?

    REQUIRED VACCINATIONS: Yellow Fever (certificate required), COVID-19 recommended, Hepatitis A & B recommended, Typhoid recommended, CRITICAL RULES DURING TRACKING: 7m minimum distance (gorillas), No coughing/sneezing toward primates, Wearing masks is optional but encouraged, No access if actively sick.

  • Are there any age restrictions for gorilla trekking or chimpanzee tracking?

    Uganda Wildlife Authority sets strict age limits for primate activities: GORILLA TREKKING: Minimum age: 15 years, No upper age limit if physically fit, CHIMPANZEE TRACKING: Minimum age: 12 years, Children 12-15 require special permission

  • What essential items should we pack specifically for primate tracking?

    CLOTHING ESSENTIALS: Waterproof hiking boots (broken in), Lightweight rain jacket (compact) Long-sleeved shirts (earth tones), Comfortable gaiters. SPECIALIZED GEAR: Portable motion sickness medication, Biodegradable wet wipes, Waterproof dry bags for electronics, Camelbak hydration pack (2L minimum). NOTE: We provide professional porters to carry heavy equipment during treks.
