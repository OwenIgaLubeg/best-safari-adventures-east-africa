Lake Mburo National Park

Safari Details

Whether you’re a traveler on a tight schedule or simply seeking a quick escape into the wild, this one-day safari to Lake Mburo National Park is the perfect way to experience Uganda’s natural beauty in a short time.

Lake Mburo offers stunning savannah landscapes, rich birdlife, and iconic wildlife such as zebras, impalas, and hippos. From scenic game drives to peaceful boat rides, this adventure is ideal for anyone looking to enjoy a memorable day in the wilderness.

End your safari at Lake Mburo where you can wind down your safari with some night-drives and lakeside relaxation.

Safari Plans

  • Day 1Arrival for the National Game Park's Adventure.

    Safari commencement – 6:00am A professional driver – guide from Hear Nature Safaris will pick you up from your desired point and start a scenic drive out of Kampala. Watch the city wake up as you cruise towards Uganda’s western countryside.

    • Uganda Equator Stopover (Kayabwe) – 8:00am (approx.) The Equator is one of the most iconic landmarks along the way to Lake Mburo, It marks the meeting point of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres, offering a unique spot for creative photography as you pose with one foot in each half of the world.
    • A local guide will demonstrate the famous Equator water experiment showing how water spirals clockwise, anti-clockwise or straight down depending on its position relative to the line, a simple but fascinating way to explain the Coriolis effect. The area also offers a vibrant cultural blend with local artisans selling handcrafted souvenirs, fabrics and jewelry adding a touch of Ugandan heritage to the scientific and scenic experience. An optional coffee refill from nearby cafes or Restaurants then head to the park.
    • Arrival at Lake Mburo National Park – 12:00am (approx.) Upon arrival, guests are welcomed into the park. Unlike most parks in Uganda, Lake Mburo allows guided walking safaris, offering a rare chance to explore the wild on foot, accompanied by an armed ranger. This nature walk reveals a deeper connection to the environment, learn to identify animal tracks, droppings and native plants while watching for Zebras, bushbucks, Topi, elands and even giraffes grazing freely. The park is also a birding haven, home to over 300 bird species including the African finfoot and red-faced barbet. This quiet immersive experience brings guests face to face with Uganda’s hidden natural rhythms.
    • Lunch at the Lodge – 1:00pm After the walk, guests unwind with a hearty lunch at a scenic lodge/restaurant such as Rwakobo lodge or Mihingo Lodge, both known for stunning views over the savannah and often frequented by wildlife. Enjoy a Fresh meal in a peaceful, elevated setting perfect for relaxing and reflecting.
    • Boat Ride on Lake Mburo – 2:30pm In the afternoon, take a tranquil boat cruise across Lake Mburo itself. This water safari offers a new perspective of the park with up-close views of hippos, crocodiles and a dazzling variety of aquatic birds like the African fish eagle, Pied Kingfisher and Papyrus gonolek. The lake’s calm surface and shoreline vegetation create excellent opportunities for wildlife photography and quiet observation.
    • Evening Game Drive – 4:00pm As the heat fades, embark on a relaxed evening game drive the best time to catch animals when they’re most active. Drive through open grasslands, rocky outcrops and acacia woodlands as you search for Zebras, Impalas, Buffaloes, Waterbucks, Warthogs and if lucky the elusive Leopard. Lake Mburo is also the only Ugandan park with Impalas and it boasts one of the highest populations of plains Zebras. The landscape glows golden as the sun sets offering perfect lighting for your final photos of the day

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What cultural experiences are included in the community visit?

    You'll experience traditional dances, witness craft-making demonstrations (pottery, basket weaving), participate in cooking local dishes, engage with village elders through storytelling sessions, and visit artisan workshops. This immersive experience supports local livelihoods through direct purchases of handmade crafts.

  • How should we dress for the cultural village visits?

    Wear modest, comfortable clothing that covers shoulders and knees as a sign of respect. Light, breathable fabrics are recommended. Avoid flashy jewelry and consider bringing a hat/sunglasses for sun protection during outdoor activities.

  • What health precautions are required for primate encounters?

    REQUIRED VACCINATIONS: Yellow Fever (certificate required), COVID-19 recommended, Hepatitis A & B recommended, Typhoid recommended, CRITICAL RULES DURING TRACKING: 7m minimum distance (gorillas), No coughing/sneezing toward primates, Wearing masks is optional but encouraged, No access if actively sick..

  • Are there any age restrictions for gorilla trekking or chimpanzee tracking?

    Uganda Wildlife Authority sets strict age limits for primate activities: GORILLA TREKKING: Minimum age: 15 years, No upper age limit if physically fit, CHIMPANZEE TRACKING: Minimum age: 12 years, Children 12-15 require special permission.

  • Are the cultural experiences suitable for children?

    Absolutely! Children particularly enjoy the interactive elements - dancing, drumming lessons, and sweet treats making. We adjust activities based on group composition, and villagers are exceptionally welcoming to young visitors.
